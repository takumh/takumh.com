The mornings had toast; crusty and golden like dawn creeping through the gaping air vents. Sometimes there was red sugarless tea: bitter like our little fights after calls that came before the six o'clock alarm: stirred the way the cream sheets looked before the missed period alarm; only sometimes.

The evenings have chocolate; decadent and dark like dusk stumbling past the undraped windows. Other times there is black distilled coffee: unperturbed the way we are, cuddled on the couch where we lie; the dregs lurking at the bottom, like the arguments that start when you lie; only other times.

The afternoons will have spaghetti: long and winding like the hours I will wait for you to come home. At times I will pour us some bubbly: effervescent the way I am after I sip wine; sweet and fruity, the wav your kisses feel after you tango and wine; only at times
