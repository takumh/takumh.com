We met where the petals fell, an incident
Under a large flowering banner, quite pungent
That had not been well planned, an accident
I said "I do a little bit of poetry" impulsively
You said "recite one for me!", compulsively
So I did, stammering, blabbering, nervously
And afterwards you cried, genuinely
The poem was trash, a tragedy
You were the only audience, a casualty
Your tears looked mocking, a travesty
But your eyes were candid, such chastity
We met where the sun set.
Under a tree where lovers met.
You in a cream dress, vellow rosette
I in a black tux, secretly breaking a sweat
I was Romeo, you Juliet
Then I woke up and realized you were a dream I would soon forget
