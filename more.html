  About Me

I grew up in Masvingo, Zimbabwe. I attended Gokomere High School for my O-levels, graduating with 12 As. I attended ZRP High School for my A levels studying Pure Mathematics, Computer Science and Physics. I graduated with 15 points (3 As equivalent). Some notable experiences from my time in Zimbabwe:

In 2017, I became the 25th President of the Junior Parliament of Zimbabwe, representing Zimbabwean youth in the country's parliament. I led a campaign to raise sanitary wear and money to purchase sanitary wear for incarcerated women and girls in orphanages. I advocated for equitable distribution of the Zimbabwe Manpower Development Fund (ZIMDEF) to all high achieving students at a time when the scholarship fund was exclusively available for students studying STEM subjects. Unfortunately, the program was discontinued altogether, unrelated to my advocacy.

In 2019, I took a gap year. For six months, I volunteered as a custodian and care giver at a local non-profit Old People's Home in Masvingo, taking care of nearly 40 residents, cleaning their quarters, mantaining their vegetable garden and grounds. I also streamlined the Home's book keeping by automating spreadsheets for budgets, donations and other records.

In 2019, after my volunteering, I worked as a registered Teaching Assistant with the Zimbabwean Ministry of Primary and Secondary Education, teaching over 250 High School students Mathematics at Maranatha High School in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe. I assisted a visually impaired teacher by conducting class and evaluating the work of students. At this post, I digitized the records of the Mathematics department, migrating physical records of past examinations, former and current students and staff records to a centralised database.
